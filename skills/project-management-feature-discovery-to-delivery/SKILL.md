# Feature Discovery to Delivery

This skill helps AI describe the path from initial discovery, through prototyping
and validation, to creating a delivery plan that the team can execute.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- plan the transition from validated discovery to delivery,
- make sure prototypes are used to reduce uncertainty,
- keep the team aligned with customer insight,
- avoid jumping into build without discovery,
- turn discovery output into a practical project plan.

---

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- explains the discovery steps required before delivery,
- clarifies when prototypes are sufficient versus when working software is needed,
- shows how to convert validated assumptions into backlog and planning,
- emphasizes customer conversations and usability testing,
- encourages a delivery plan rooted in discovered evidence.

---

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Describe discovery activities**
   - Recommend framing the feature idea and validating it with customer conversations.
   - Explain the role of prototypes in testing value and usability.
   - Advise teams to use discovery to reduce the biggest unknowns.

2. **Explain prototype fidelity decisions**
   - Recommend simple prototypes when they can answer the right questions.
   - Advise against building working software too early if a simple prototype suffices.
   - Emphasize that the goal is learning, not polished output.

3. **Convert discovery into planning**
   - Recommend creating a backlog and development plan only after validation.
   - Advise teams to use the discovery artifacts to tell the feature story.
   - Encourage including technical dependencies and risk findings in the plan.

4. **Keep the team informed**
   - Recommend sharing the story map and prototypes with the delivery team.
   - Advise the team to use the shared understanding to estimate and scope work.
   - Encourage the team to capture questions and assumptions as they go.

---

## Quality criteria

A strong discovery-to-delivery recommendation should be:

- **evidence-based:** planning follows validated discovery,
- **customer-informed:** user needs and usability insights drive decisions,
- **pragmatic:** prototypes are used appropriately,
- **aligned:** delivery work reflects the validated solution,
- **transparent:** the team understands what was learned and why.

---

## Example prompts

- "How do we move from prototyping to a delivery plan?"
- "When is a simple prototype enough before we start building?"
- "How should we turn customer discovery into a feature backlog?"
