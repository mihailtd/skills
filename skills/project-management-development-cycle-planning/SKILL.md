# Development Cycle Planning

This skill helps AI explain how to plan development cycles without torture, how to prepare stories ahead of time, and how to use maps to keep the team connected to the product.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- make planning sessions shorter and more effective,
- choose stories for the next cycle with enough lead time,
- help teams plan in small groups with the right participants,
- use a story map to keep the big picture visible during delivery,
- visualize progress and help stakeholders understand what’s happening.

---

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- encourages preparing stories one or two cycles ahead,
- recommends story workshops before planning sessions,
- advocates small-group planning rather than crowd-based meetings,
- explains how to use a map as a delivery dashboard and context tool,
- warns against trying to do opportunity discovery, delivery strategy, and detailed planning in one meeting,
- promotes shared ownership of the cycle plan and predictable commitments.

---

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Prepare before planning**
   - Recommend choosing candidate stories ahead of the cycle.
   - Explain that product and delivery teams should workshop stories before the planning meeting.
   - Advise inviting the whole team but keeping the actual planning work small and focused.

2. **Plan in small groups**
   - Describe how the team can split into small groups to size and sequence stories.
   - Recommend the product owner, designers, developers, and testers stay available to answer questions.
   - Explain that the group should agree on what the team believes it can complete in the cycle.

3. **Use timeboxes and focus**
   - Encourage setting a timebox for planning the stories and for the whole cycle planning session.
   - Warn against letting planning become a long, painful crowd event.
   - Explain that planning is better when people can opt in and participate actively.

4. **Recognize progressive conversation stages**
   - Explain that opportunity assessment, discovery, delivery strategy, and last-best story workshops each have a different purpose.
   - Warn against trying to cover all of those conversations in one sitting.
   - Recommend keeping the right people in the right conversations as stories move from big ideas to build-ready work.

5. **Visualize progress with maps**
   - Recommend using story maps as a big-picture product context tool during delivery.
   - Explain how teams can mark completed stories or remaining stories on the map.
   - Advise using the map to choose the next stories, identify gaps, and keep momentum visible.

6. **Treat the plan as a commitment**
   - Encourage the team to take the agreed plan seriously and use it to build trust.
   - Explain that if the plan doesn’t fit, they should slice stories thinner or reduce scope.
   - Recommend celebrating the completion of planning and starting the cycle refreshed.

---

## Decision points and guidance

- **Are stories ready enough for planning?** If no, workshop them first.
- **Is the planning group too large or disengaged?** If yes, shrink the active conversation and use observers.
- **Does the team understand the product context?** If no, use a map to show the big picture.
- **Are we trying to do opportunity discovery and detailed cycle planning in the same meeting?** If yes, split that work across the proper stages.
- **Does the cycle plan fit the available time?** If no, thin stories or reduce scope.
- **Is the team agreeing just to end the meeting?** If yes, pause and refocus on real plan quality.

---

## Quality criteria

A strong development-cycle-planning response should ensure the team is:

- **prepared:** stories are workshop-ready before planning,
- **focused:** the planning conversation is small and active,
- **visual:** the map keeps the product visible,
- **realistic:** the plan matches the cycle capacity,
- **collaborative:** everyone understands how the stories fit together,
- **positive:** the end of planning is a celebration of agreement.
