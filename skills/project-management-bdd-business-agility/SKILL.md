# BDD and Business Agility

This skill helps AI explain how Behavior-Driven Development (BDD) and business agility work together to keep teams focused on outcomes, learning, and measurable business value.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- explain the business agility case for BDD,
- help teams understand why BDD is not only about tests,
- connect business vision and goals to executable specifications,
- show how BDD supports fast feedback and continuous learning,
- emphasize value, collaboration, and living documentation.

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- defines business agility in terms of adaptability, responsiveness, and sustainable advantage,
- positions BDD as a collaborative way to discover the right work and minimize waste,
- describes how executable examples make conversations concrete,
- explains why BDD helps teams focus on business impact rather than only technical output,
- encourages teams to treat features as hypotheses that can be validated.

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Define business agility clearly**
   - Mention that it is the ability to adapt quickly to market changes, respond to customer demands, and continuously learn.
   - Emphasize that agility is as much about behavior and capability as it is about process.
   - Note that the goal is better outcomes and better wellbeing, not just faster delivery.

2. **Describe the role of BDD in that context**
   - Explain that BDD gives teams techniques to collaborate at strategic and delivery levels.
   - Show that BDD helps turn business goals into shared examples and executable specifications.
   - Reinforce that this alignment reduces the risk of building the wrong thing.

3. **Explain the hypothesis mindset**
   - Introduce the “We believe... We will know it’s true when...” formula.
   - Describe how features are bets that should be tested with measurable outcomes.
   - Encourage using metrics and success criteria to validate whether work delivered real business value.

4. **Make examples practical**
   - Use a generic scenario such as a loyalty or product experience project to illustrate how business goals become examples.
   - Show how business value might be expressed as increasing sales, improving retention, or reducing costs.
   - Mention that features should be phrased in terms of capabilities and outcomes rather than technical solutions.

5. **Highlight collaboration and learning loops**
   - Advise involving stakeholders, product people, developers, and testers in conversations.
   - Explain that BDD creates living documentation that evolves with the product.
   - Stress that feedback from delivered work should feed back into the next Speculate phase.

6. **Warn about common BDD misconceptions**
   - Call out the idea that BDD is just a testing framework or a form of automation.
   - Explain that automation is only one benefit; the main value is shared understanding and alignment.
   - Advise against letting example formats become more important than the business conversation.

## Decision points and guidance

- **If the user asks about testing tools:** keep the response focused on the collaboration and value aspects of BDD first.
- **If the audience is leadership:** emphasize outcomes, agility, and investment return.
- **If the audience is delivery teams:** emphasize how BDD reduces rework and keeps teams aligned with business goals.
- **If the user asks about documentation:** recommend living examples over static requirements.

## Quality criteria

A strong BDD and business agility response should be:

- **outcome-oriented:** it emphasizes business value first,
- **collaborative:** it shows how people work together across roles,
- **experimental:** it treats work as hypotheses to be validated,
- **iterative:** it shows feedback loops between Speculate and Validate,
- **explanatory:** it distinguishes BDD from mere automation.

## Example prompts

- "How does BDD help an organization become more business agile?"
- "Why should our team use examples instead of writing detailed requirements?"
- "What is the relationship between business goals and BDD scenarios?"
