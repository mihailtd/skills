# Risk Story Mapping

This skill helps AI recommend how to add explicit risk stories to story maps,
so teams can visualize uncertainty, plan for learning, and manage delivery risk.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- plan work with both known stories and risk-related learning work,
- surface uncertainty as part of the delivery plan,
- improve stakeholder visibility into risk and complexity,
- create a risk burn-down alongside feature burn-down,
- make story maps reflect actual effort, not just known scope.

---

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- explains the value of risk stories within a story map,
- shows how risk stories reveal hidden work and uncertainties,
- recommends updating the map frequently with new risks,
- connects risk discovery to better planning and predictability,
- helps teams communicate risk alongside feature progress.

---

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Explain risk stories**
   - Define a risk story as a planned exploration or mitigation task.
   - Explain that risk stories are distinct from normal feature stories.
   - Encourage teams to include risks that affect delivery, quality, or assumptions.

2. **Integrate risks into the story map**
   - Advise placing risk stories alongside the normal map structure.
   - Recommend visual cues or separate swimlanes to make risk visible.
   - Stress that the map should show both known work and the learning work needed to make it predictable.

3. **Use risk burn-downs**
   - Recommend tracking how risk stories are completed over time.
   - Explain how risk burn-down adds context when feature burn-down alone looks good.
   - Suggest using risk burn-down to update stakeholders on learning progress.

4. **Update the map regularly**
   - Encourage frequent reviews to capture new unknowns.
   - Advise the team to add risk stories as they discover uncertainty.
   - Recommend revisiting risk priority as the project evolves.

5. **Balance risk and feature work**
   - Recommend that the team plan for risk work alongside feature delivery.
   - Warn against ignoring risk stories because they do not immediately produce visible user value.

---

## Quality criteria

A strong risk story mapping recommendation should be:

- **visible:** risk appears clearly on the map,
- **explicit:** uncertainty is treated as planned work,
- **measurable:** risk progress is tracked over time,
- **actionable:** risk stories drive better planning,
- **adaptive:** the plan evolves as new unknowns are discovered.

---

## Example prompts

- "How can we make our story map show both feature work and delivery risk?"
- "Explain how risk stories help improve our planning accuracy."
- "What should a risk burn-down chart show alongside feature progress?"
