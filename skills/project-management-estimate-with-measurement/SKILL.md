# Estimate with Measurement

This skill helps AI explain how teams can get better at estimating by measuring
how long small pieces of work actually take and using that data to inform future plans.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- improve estimation accuracy over time,
- treat estimates as a budget rather than a guarantee,
- build a feedback loop from real delivery data,
- make planning more predictable with frequent measurement,
- help teams learn from their own historical performance.

---

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- treats estimates as informed guesses subject to measurement,
- encourages measuring the time spent on small, comparable pieces,
- shows how frequent measurement improves prediction,
- explains the value of a delivery budget and budget management,
- helps teams use data to manage scope and expectations.

---

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Emphasize estimate humility**
   - Explain that estimates are not exact predictions.
   - Recommend communicating them as budgets or forecasts.
   - Warn against treating estimates as commitments without data.

2. **Recommend frequent measurement**
   - Advise teams to track how long small pieces actually take.
   - Explain that more measurements increase confidence in future estimates.
   - Use the commuting analogy: repeated similar tasks improve prediction.

3. **Use measurement to improve planning**
   - Recommend comparing actuals to estimates regularly.
   - Advise teams to adjust future plans based on measured performance.
   - Encourage using small slices so that measurement is meaningful and frequent.

4. **Manage the delivery budget**
   - Recommend treating early estimates as a spending budget.
   - Explain how teams can borrow budget, adjust scope, or reset expectations.
   - Emphasize that awareness of variance is more valuable than surprise.

---

## Quality criteria

A strong estimate-with-measurement recommendation should be:

- **realistic:** estimates are treated as approximate budgets,
- **data-driven:** actual work durations inform future estimates,
- **frequent:** measurement happens often enough to learn,
- **actionable:** the team uses the data to adjust plans,
- **transparent:** the budget and performance are visible to the team.

---

## Example prompts

- "How can we get better at estimating by measuring real work?"
- "What does it mean to treat development estimates as a delivery budget?"
- "How often should a team measure actual delivery time to improve predictions?"
