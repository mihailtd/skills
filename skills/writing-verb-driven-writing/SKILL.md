# Verb-Driven Writing and Review

This skill guides the review and improvement of writing (fiction, non-fiction, copywriting, journalism) by applying the principle: "Honor the verb, sacrifice the adjective."

## Core Principles

1.  **Use Verbs of Manner**: English possesses hundreds of specific verbs (e.g., *zoom, waddle, zigzag, hurl, sprint*). Use them to express the nuance, speed, force, and feeling of an action instead of relying on basic verbs paired with adjectives or adverbs.
2.  **Avoid Nominalizations**: Do not turn perfectly good verbs into heavy, stodgy nouns. 
    *   *Instead of:* "represents an evening of celebration, connection and recognition"
    *   *Do this:* "celebrate what we've achieved... Connect with members"
3.  **Subject-Verb Proximity**: Start sentences with the subject and verb close together. Fuzzy, meandering phrasing confuses readers.
4.  **Intentional Tone**: 
    *   Use bold, descriptive "verbs of manner" for drama, energy, and specific action.
    *   Use basic verbs (*walk, drop, go*) or stative verbs (*to be, seem, know*) when a calm, intimate, or objective tone is genuinely desired. Do not use them by default.

## Patterns & Anti-Patterns

### Anti-Pattern 1: The Adjective/Adverb Crutch
*   **Weak:** The dog ran very fast out the door.
*   **Strong:** The dog zoomed out the door.

### Anti-Pattern 2: The Stodgy Noun (Nominalization)
*   **Weak:** We will make the announcement on social media.
*   **Strong:** We will announce it on social media.

### Anti-Pattern 3: The Meandering Passive or Vague State
*   **Weak:** It has been determined that current methods are not suitable for use as a means to directly reduce numbers...
*   **Strong:** Current methods won't work right now to limit numbers...

## Writing Review Checklist

When reviewing a piece of text, execute the following steps:

- [ ] **Scan for Stative Verbs:** Highlight uses of *is, are, was, were, seems, appears*. Can any of these be replaced with a dynamic action?
- [ ] **Eradicate Nominalizations:** Find nouns ending in *-tion, -ment, -ance, -ity*. Convert them back into their root verbs if they are weighing down the sentence.
- [ ] **Check Subject-Verb Proximity:** Ensure the actor and the action are close to the beginning of the sentence.
- [ ] **Upgrade Basic Verbs:** Identify basic verbs (*walk, say, look, go*) and ask if a "verb of manner" (*strut, roar, glare, dart*) would be more truthful and evocative.
- [ ] **Assess Tone:** Does the choice of verbs match the intended tone? (e.g., simple/blunt for fast pacing, complex/manner for imagery, basic for calm neutrality).
- [ ] **Eliminate Clutter:** Remove passive voices, bureaucratic phrasing, and "filler" wording that buries the actual action.

## Usage
