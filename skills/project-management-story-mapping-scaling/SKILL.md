# Story Mapping at Scale

This skill helps AI recommend how to scale story mapping in larger product
organizations, with coaching, structured workshops, and clear practices for
handling complexity.

---

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- deploy story mapping beyond a single team,
- run story mapping workshops with many stakeholders,
- handle complex products without losing clarity,
- highlight open questions, dependencies, and risks on the map,
- train others to use story mapping successfully.

---

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- describes a coach-supported workshop approach,
- explains how to combine vision, personas, and story mapping,
- recommends a three-tier story breakdown for larger initiatives,
- shows how to visualize open issues and knowledge gaps,
- supports scaling through templates, training, and communities of practice.

---

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Recommend workshop preparation**
   - Advise starting with a clear workshop goal and invite the right people.
   - Recommend preparatory work such as product vision or elevator pitch exercises.
   - Explain the value of involving a coach or experienced facilitator.

2. **Describe the three-tier mapping approach**
   - Recommend starting with high-level usage steps.
   - Then drill down into persona-specific activities.
   - Finally break those into concrete user stories where needed.

3. **Encourage role and persona clarity**
   - Advise mapping user roles or personas based on research or assumptions.
   - Explain that color-coding by role helps teams see who the map serves.
   - Recommend testing assumptions when user research is incomplete.

4. **Highlight unknowns and open issues**
   - Recommend using separate sticky colors or shapes for questions, dependencies, and gaps.
   - Explain that visible open issues make the map more honest and useful.
   - Advise teams to capture what needs more research and revisit it later.

5. **Describe prioritization and alignment**
   - Recommend simple voting or Kano-style categories for prioritizing stories.
   - Explain that prioritization can happen at the activity or story level.
   - Advise using results as alignment tools with stakeholders and customers.

6. **Support scaling with coaching and artifacts**
   - Recommend training coaches through apprenticeship and shadowing.
   - Suggest creating standard workshop agendas, templates, and method cheat sheets.
   - Encourage communities of practice and shared learning across locations.

---

## Quality criteria

A strong scaling recommendation should be:

- **structured:** the approach works for larger teams and products,
- **facilitated:** coaching supports consistent outcomes,
- **role-aware:** personas and roles are explicit,
- **transparent:** risks and open questions are visible,
- **scalable:** practices can be adopted by more teams over time.
