# Snappy Verbing (Denominalization)

## Goal
Transform dull, wordy descriptions into snappy, innovative verbs by turning nouns into verbs (verbing/denominalizing). This editor cuts back on words and injects fresh energy by deploying instantly understandable "verbed" nouns, while strictly hunting down and destroying awkward, multi-syllable corporate freak-verbs (like *functionalize* or *diligence*).

## Checklist for Writing Review
- [ ] **Spot Compression Opportunities**: Look for phrases that can be condensed into a single, punchy verbed noun (e.g., instead of "make a search on Google", use "Google").
- [ ] **The "Kitchen-Table Test"**: Check any newly invented verb. Will an average reader instantly understand it without an explanation based on context and common knowledge? If it requires "mental squinting," kill it.
- [ ] **Eradicate Syllable-Stacking (Corporate Freak-Verbs)**: Scan for bloated, multi-syllable monstrosities ending in *-ize*, *-ate*, or *-ify* (e.g., *securitize*, *deconfessionalize*). Replace them with clear, simple actions.
- [ ] **Check the Context**: Ensure the surrounding sentences clearly stage the verbed noun so it lands flawlessly (e.g., "Emily therapized for that" works because the prior sentence was about a bad breakup).
- [ ] **Clarify Metaphorical Verbing**: When verbing a noun to create a metaphor, ensure the visualization is correct (e.g., "her arms windmilled" is clear; "her arms wheelbarrowed" is confusing).

## Anti-patterns (What to Avoid)
- **Corporate/Legal Jargon Verbs**: Masking a simple action behind a string of Latinate syllables.
  - *Bad*: "We need to functionalize this." / "He diligenced the assets." / "We nunc pro tunced it."
- **Mental Squinting**: Inventing a verb that forces the reader to guess the connection.
  - *Bad*: "Let's casserole the assets." / "She wheelbarrowed across the room."
- **Vague Abstractions**: Turning a complicated noun into a verb that papers over actual clear steps.
  - *Bad*: "He demagogued the crowd." (It's too vague—what did he actually do?)

## Good Patterns (What to Do)
- **Economical Cultural Shorthand**: Using widely understood brands or cultural touchstones as verbs.
  - *Good*: "I Ubered home." / "She Venmoed the rent." / "Let's MacGyver a solution."
- **Vivid, Instant Visuals**: Turning a noun with a clear physical trait into an action.
  - *Good*: "He windmilled his arms." / "They knifed through the waves." / "He buttered up the boss."
- **Context-Fueled Inventions**: Making up a one-off verb that fits perfectly in the specific scenario you've built.
  - *Good*: "The pope is poping beautifully." / "People be peopling." / "That attempts to giddyap his career."

## Examples

### Example 1: Killing Corporate Jargon Verbs
**Original:** To date, the firm has sourced, structured, diligenced, and securitized the financing.
**Edited:** To date, the firm has sourced, structured, investigated, and secured the financing. 

### Example 2: Compressing Wordy Descriptions into Verbed Nouns
**Original:** Emily went off to ride her Harley motorcycle out of the Blue Ridge Parkway.
**Edited:** Emily skipped off and Harleyed the hell out of the Blue Ridge Parkway.

### Example 3: Fixing "Mental Squinting"
**Original:** I tried to napkin up the mistake before the boss saw it.
**Edited:** I tried to sponge up the mistake before the boss saw it.
