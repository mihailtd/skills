# Deliberate Discovery

This skill helps AI explain how teams can proactively uncover uncertainty, learn what they need to know, and reduce the risk of ignorance before making feature decisions.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill when you need to:

- explain why ignorance is a primary risk in software development,
- show how teams can deliberately discover unknowns before committing,
- recommend practices for identifying and reducing uncertainty,
- connect discovery work to BDD scenarios and refinement,
- help teams choose which stories to explore first.

## Outcome

Produce guidance that:

- defines Deliberate Discovery as the opposite of blind commitment,
- explains that uncertainty should be surfaced and reduced proactively,
- recommends tackling the riskiest, most uncertain stories early,
- ties discovery to better backlog refinement and release planning,
- positions discovery work as an investment that enables smarter decisions.

## Instructions for the AI

1. **Explain the role of ignorance**
   - Describe ignorance as the constraint that makes software decisions risky.
   - Explain that Deliberate Discovery acknowledges unknowns rather than pretending they do not exist.
   - Reinforce that the goal is to reduce uncertainty before costly implementation choices are made.

2. **Describe discovery practices**
   - Recommend identifying unknowns early in backlog refinement and story breakdown.
   - Suggest using experiments, prototypes, spikes, research, and stakeholder conversations.
   - Advise defining specific questions or hypotheses the team needs to answer.

3. **Prioritize uncertainty reduction**
   - Recommend tackling the highest-risk stories first, not necessarily the easiest.
   - Explain that learning early makes later planning more reliable.
   - Mention that this habit helps make the last responsible moment meaningful.

4. **Connect discovery to BDD**
   - Show how examples and scenarios can be used to surface ambiguity.
   - Explain that BDD conversations are discovery activities that reveal assumptions.
   - Recommend capturing what is known, what is uncertain, and what needs validation.

5. **Advise on practical workflow**
   - Suggest setting aside deliberate discovery time as part of refinement or early sprint planning.
   - Recommend keeping discovery outcomes visible, such as through simple notes, spike stories, or decision logs.
   - Advise balancing discovery work with delivery work so the team still makes progress.

## Decision points and guidance

- **If the team is unsure what to build:** recommend discovery work before committing to a solution.
- **If stories feel too brittle:** suggest exploring assumptions with examples and small experiments.
- **If the backlog has many unknowns:** help the team categorize them by risk and impact.
- **If asked about timeboxing:** explain that discovery should be small, focused, and evidence-driven.

## Quality criteria

A strong Deliberate Discovery response should be:

- **aware:** it accepts uncertainty as a normal part of software work,
- **targeted:** it focuses on the riskiest unknowns first,
- **collaborative:** it uses conversation and experimentation,
- **visible:** it makes assumptions and questions explicit,
- **balanced:** it keeps discovery lightweight while improving confidence.
